⚖️ Equity, Inclusion & Local Partnership

Why funders care USAID's new "Localisation Agenda" (2022) requires that at least 25% of funds go directly to local organisations. FCDO's "locally owned" criterion is now weighted at 20% in most solicitations. Gates Foundation's "gender equality and inclusion" is mandatory across all health and development grants. 💰 Value for Money & Budget Quality

—
Why funders care FCDO's Smart Rules mandate a formal VfM (Value for Money) assessment using the 3Es framework: Economy (low input costs), Efficiency (outputs per pound), Effectiveness (outcomes per output). USAID uses a "Cost Analysis" section in most solicitations. The World Bank uses cost-benefit analysis. A compelling VfM case can offset weaknesses in other areas — evaluators are trained that a cheap, well-evidenced intervention beats an expensive, uncertain one.

♻️ Sustainability & Scale Potential

—
Why funders care All major donors are under pressure to demonstrate that grants "exit" successfully. USAID's CDCS framework requires a "transition plan." FCDO Smart Rules: "describe how benefits will continue after funding ends." Gates Foundation scales up only if "the approach can be taken to national or global scale." MacArthur 100&Change specifically requires a "path to durability." Strong sustainability cases involve: government co-investment, policy change, or revenue-generation — not just "we'll seek more grants."
