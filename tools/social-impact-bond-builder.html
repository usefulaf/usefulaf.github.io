Contract Basics ℹ SIB: private investors fund services, government repays if outcomes achieved. DIB: donors fund, repay based on development outcomes. Outcome Contract: direct commissioner-provider agreement with payment on results.
SIBs: private capital at risk. DIBs: donor/government repayment. OBCs: no external investors.
Commissioner
E.g. reduced custody, healthcare, benefits costs. Source: government actuarial data.
Risk Allocation ℹ In a SIB, investors bear performance risk (non-payment if outcomes not met). Commissioners bear basis risk (outcome metrics don't capture true impact). Service providers bear delivery risk.

Outcome Metrics & Payment Triggers ℹ Define measurable outcomes that trigger payment. Best practice (based on GO Lab, Brookings): use 2-4 metrics, combine process + impact outcomes, ensure data routinely available via administrative data.
📊 GO Lab benchmark: UK SIBs average 3.2 outcome metrics. Most use a tiered payment structure (floor, base, stretch). Administrative data (e.g. DWP, NHS records) is preferred over primary evaluation.
