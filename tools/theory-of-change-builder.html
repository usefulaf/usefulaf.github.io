Theory of Change Builder Useful AF Tool

Build a structured, evidence-informed Theory of Change for your programme or organisation

A Theory of Change (ToC) maps how and why your activities will lead to the change you want to see. Complete each section below and this tool will generate a visual outcomes chain, suggested indicators, key assumptions, and a ready-to-use Claude prompt for deeper analysis.

1 Context & Problem Statement

Define the problem you're addressing and the population you serve.
Press Enter after each cause. Example: "No grid infrastructure", "High upfront equipment cost", "Limited financial literacy"

2 Inputs

What resources are you investing? (funding, staff, partnerships, technology, knowledge)
    Examples: Grant funding, Loan capital, Staff (FTEs), Volunteer hours, Partner networks, Technology, Land/facilities, Community trust

    3 Activities

    What will you actually do? List the key programme activities.
      Examples: Training workshops, Equipment installation, Loans disbursed, Advocacy campaigns, Research, Case management, Construction

      4 Outputs

      Direct, measurable products of your activities — the things you can count immediately.
        Outputs are what you produce, not what changes. Example: "200 people trained" (output) vs "200 people employed" (outcome)

        5 Outcomes

        Changes in people, communities, or systems. Short-term (1–2 yrs) lead to long-term (3–5 yrs).

            6 Impact

            The long-term, systemic change you contribute to — usually beyond your direct control.

            7 Key Assumptions & Risks

            What must be true for your Theory of Change to hold? What could go wrong?
