About the 2X Challenge: The 2X Challenge is the world's largest gender-lens investment initiative, mobilising capital from DFIs to businesses that advance gender equity across five dimensions: ownership, leadership, employment quality, supply chain, and products/services. To qualify for 2X recognition, a company must meet at least one of the five criteria thresholds.
Source: 2X Challenge 2.0 Criteria (2023), IFC/DFI Development Finance Institutions

Women's ownership and equity stake. 2X Threshold: ≥51% women-owned or a women-led fund/vehicle.
2X Threshold: ≥51% women-owned OR women-led investment vehicle

Dimension 2: Leadership
Women in senior decision-making roles. 2X Thresholds: ≥30% women on Board or ≥30% women in C-suite/Senior Management.
2X Threshold: ≥30% women on Board OR ≥30% women in Senior Management
% women on Board of Directors
?
Count all board seats including independent directors. Exclude advisory boards — count only voting board members. 30% is the "critical mass" threshold at which women meaningfully influence decisions. Source: 2X Challenge; McKinsey Women in the Workplace 2023
Include independent, executive, and non-executive directors with voting rights. Exclude advisors.
📋 2X Challenge 2.0; IFC Corporate Governance Methodology (2019)
% women in C-suite / Senior Management
?
Senior management = CEO, CFO, COO, CMO, CTO, VP/Director level with P&L responsibility or 5+ direct reports. Do not count middle management. Source: 2X Challenge 2.0 Criteria; ILO World Employment and Social Outlook 2023
C-suite and VP/Director level roles with budget authority or 5+ direct reports.
📋 2X Challenge 2.0; ILO WESR 2023; McKinsey WitW 2023
Is the CEO a woman?
?
CEO gender has an outsized effect on gender equity outcomes throughout the organisation. Companies with women CEOs have measurably higher women representation at all levels. Source: McKinsey Women in the Workplace 2023; IFC (2020)
% women in middle management
Managers with ≥1 direct report but below senior management level. The "broken rung" — this is where women's advancement most commonly stalls. Source: McKinsey WitW 2023.

Dimension 3: Employment Quality
Quality of employment for women workers. 2X Threshold: ≥40% women in workforce AND at least 2 qualifying employment quality indicators met.
2X Threshold: ≥40% women workforce AND ≥2 quality indicators
Quality indicators count only if the company also meets the ≥40% women workforce threshold. Employment quality is assessed against IFC Performance Standard 2 (Labour & Working Conditions) and ILO Decent Work principles.
👥 Workforce Composition
% women in total workforce
All employees, full-time and part-time. For agriculture/seasonal: use peak season employment. Source: 2X Challenge 2.0; ILO Labour Force Statistics 2023.
% women in workforce
⭐ Employment Quality Indicators (need ≥2 to qualify)
Equal pay / pay equity policy
?
A formal written policy committing to equal pay for equal work. Best practice: conduct regular pay equity audits and publish the gender pay gap. Global gender pay gap = ~18% (ILO 2023). A formal policy without audit is "partial". Source: ILO Equal Remuneration Convention (C100); World Economic Forum Global Gender Gap Report 2024
📋 ILO Convention C100; WEF Global Gender Gap Report 2024 (18% global gap)
Parental leave policy (beyond legal minimum)
?
Legal minimums vary: 14 weeks maternity (ILO C183), 10 days paternity (EU minimum). Qualifying = at least 16 weeks fully paid maternity AND ≥2 weeks paid paternity. Global benchmark: Scandinavian standard = 12+ months combined. Source: ILO Maternity Protection Convention C183 (2000); World Policy Analysis Center 2023
📋 ILO Maternity Protection C183; World Policy Analysis Center (2023)
Safe & harassment-free workplace
?
ILO C190 Violence and Harassment Convention (2019) requires policies covering all workers. Qualifying standard: written PSHEA policy + confidential reporting mechanism + regular training + zero-tolerance stance. Source: ILO C190 (2019); IFC PS2
📋 ILO C190 (2019); IFC Performance Standard 2; UN Women Safe Cities
Childcare support
?
Childcare is the single greatest barrier to women's workforce participation globally. IFC recommends employers in sectors with >30% women workers to provide childcare support. Qualifying = on-site childcare OR childcare subsidy ≥15% of local average childcare cost. Source: IFC (2019); World Bank WBL Index 2024
📋 IFC Childcare in the Workplace (2019); World Bank WBL 2024
Flexible work / remote work options
Formal flexible working policies (flex hours, remote/hybrid, part-time pathways to promotion). Source: ILO Telework Guidance 2020; McKinsey WitW 2023 (flexibility valued by 87% of women workers).
Training & skills development for women
Targeted training programmes, mentorship, or sponsorship for women's career advancement. Source: ILO Skills for the Future of Work (2021); UN Women WEPs.

Dimension 4: Supply Chain
Women's inclusion in the supply chain as producers, suppliers, distributors, or agents. 2X Threshold: ≥25% of supply chain spend with women-owned businesses OR ≥50% women in farmer/producer network.
2X Threshold: ≥25% women-owned suppliers OR ≥50% women farmers/producers/agents
% of procurement/supply chain spend with women-owned businesses
?
Women-owned = ≥51% women-owned and managed. Qualifying spend includes goods, services, and raw material suppliers. Exclude utilities and government procurement. Source: 2X Challenge 2.0; IFC Women-Owned SMEs Toolkit (2020); WEConnect International standards
📋 2X Challenge 2.0; WEConnect International; IFC Women-Owned SMEs (2020)
% spend with women-owned suppliers
% women in farmer / producer / distributor / agent network
?
Applies to agriculture, financial services (agents), FMCG (distributors), and similar models. 50% threshold reflects population parity. Source: 2X Challenge; CGAP (women's financial inclusion in agent networks reaches 40-60% in leading programmes)
📋 2X Challenge 2.0; CGAP Financial Inclusion Data 2023; FAO State of Food & Agriculture 2023
% women in producer/agent network (enter 0 if N/A)
Active supplier diversity programme
Formal programme to identify, develop and prefer women-owned suppliers. Includes capacity building for women suppliers. Source: WEConnect International; IFC (2020).
Supply chain due diligence on gender risks
Does the company assess gender-based risks (PSHEA, child labour, forced labour, wage gaps) in its supply chain? IFC PS2 and ILO MNE Declaration require this for Tier 1 suppliers at minimum. Source: IFC PS2; UN Guiding Principles on Business & Human Rights (UNGPs).

Dimension 5: Products & Services
Whether the company's core products/services intentionally address women's needs or advance gender equity. 2X Threshold: ≥50% of revenues from products/services designed for or with women, AND they demonstrably improve wellbeing.
2X Threshold: ≥50% revenue from products designed for women AND demonstrated wellbeing benefit
% of revenue from products/services primarily designed for women
?
Products must be intentionally designed to address women's specific needs, not just purchased by women. E.g.: maternal health services, women's financial products (credit, insurance), women's agricultural inputs, femtech, gender-based violence support services. Source: 2X Challenge 2.0; USAID WomenConnect Challenge
📋 2X Challenge 2.0; USAID WomenConnect; CGAP Women's Financial Inclusion Report 2023
% revenue from women-targeted products
Evidence of wellbeing improvement for women users/customers
Has the company collected data showing its products improve women's financial, physical, social, or economic outcomes? IMP framework: evidence level (anecdotal → survey → RCT). Source: 2X Challenge 2.0; GIIN IRIS+ PD 7375.
📋 2X Challenge 2.0; GIIN IRIS+ PD 7375; IMP Evidence Requirements
Women co-designed or consulted in product development
Human-centred design with women beneficiaries. Source: IDEO.org HCD principles; CGAP human-centred design for financial inclusion (2015).
Affordability and accessibility for low-income women
Is the product accessible to women in the bottom 40% of the income distribution? Price, distribution channel, language/literacy, digital access. Source: CGAP Financial Inclusion (2023); World Bank Global Findex 2022.
📋 World Bank Global Findex 2022; CGAP 2023; ITU Digital Gender Gap 2023
